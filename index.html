The Problem

Blood banks operate blind.

Demand spikes are unpredictable. Inventory expires. Donors churn. Regulatory audits are manual. Each of these failures costs lives and millions — yet the industry still runs on spreadsheets and intuition.

Demand is unforecastable
Surgical schedules, trauma events, and seasonal disease cycles create unpredictable surges. Banks have no predictive layer — they react after shortages hit.
Inventory expires at scale
Red blood cells have a 42-day shelf life. Platelets, just 5 days. Without demand-matched allocation, banks routinely discard units worth thousands of dollars.
Donor retention is collapsing
Post-pandemic donor pools are thinning. Banks can't identify at-risk donors before they lapse, spending heavily on re-acquisition instead of retention.
Regulatory traceability is manual
FDA 21 CFR Part 606 and EU Blood Directive audits require full chain-of-custody. Generating these records currently takes days of manual effort per audit.

The Platform

Four modules.
One supply chain.

Each module works standalone or as a unified system across blood banks and plasma centers.

Core Module

Demand Forecasting Engine

Predicts blood product demand 7–21 days ahead at blood type and product-level granularity. Integrates hospital surgical schedules, emergency intake patterns, and seasonal disease epidemiology.

  • Blood type–level demand by product category (RBC, platelets, plasma, cryoprecipitate)
  • Surgical calendar integration for elective procedure planning
  • Seasonal and regional disease signal ingestion
  • Cross-facility redistribution recommendations before expiry
Inventory Intelligence

Expiry Minimisation Layer

Tracks every unit in real-time against forecasted demand, surfacing redistribution and issuing prioritization decisions that cut discard rates by up to 30%.

  • Unit-level shelf life countdown against demand forecast
  • Automated redistribution alerts across hospital and blood bank network
  • Issuing-order optimization by expiry and patient compatibility
  • Waste attribution reporting for quality teams
Donor Intelligence

Donor Retention Prediction

Identifies donors at risk of lapsing before they do, enabling proactive outreach campaigns and optimizing collection drive targeting for maximum yield per contact.

  • Lapse-risk scoring across entire donor registry
  • Segmented re-engagement campaign triggers
  • Collection drive geo-targeting by blood type scarcity
  • Lifetime donor value modeling for recruitment ROI
Compliance Automation

Regulatory Traceability Suite

Full chain-of-custody logging from donor identification through transfusion, generating FDA 21 CFR Part 606 and EU Blood Directive–compliant reports automatically.

  • Real-time chain-of-custody ledger per unit
  • One-click audit report generation for FDA, AABB, EU regulatory bodies
  • Adverse event traceability and donor lookback automation
  • Immutable audit trail with role-based access controls
Why Now

The window is open.

Three convergent shifts are making this the only viable moment to build.

01
Post-pandemic donor collapse
COVID-19 permanently thinned donor pools. ARC and WHO both flagged chronic shortages. Blood banks are now actively investing in predictive systems to compensate for reduced supply buffers.
02
LIS systems are finally API-ready
Blood bank software modernization (Mediware, Soft Systems, Epic LIS) over 2019–2024 has created structured, accessible data layers. AI integration that was technically impossible 5 years ago is now straightforward.
03
Regulatory pressure is accelerating
FDA and EU regulators increased digital traceability requirements post-2021. Manual compliance is no longer acceptable. Automation is shifting from a "nice to have" to a license-to-operate requirement.
